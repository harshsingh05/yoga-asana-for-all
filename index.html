Yoga Asanas for All Ages

Discover a world of wellness through mindful movement.

For Little Yogis (Ages 3-12)

These fun and imaginative poses are perfect for building strength, flexibility, and body awareness in children.

1. Mountain Pose (Tadasana)

Standing tall and still like a strong mountain, feeling grounded and steady.

2. Tree Pose (Vrksasana)

Balance on one leg, letting your body sway gently like a tree in the breeze.

3. Dog Pose (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

Form an inverted V-shape, stretching like a happy dog waking up!

4. Cat Pose (Marjaryasana)

Round your back like a spooky Halloween cat, then arch it like a sleepy feline.

5. Cow Pose (Bitilasana)

Drop your belly and look up like a gentle cow grazing in a field.

6. Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana)

Lift your chest like a curious cobra, keeping your hips on the ground.

7. Butterfly Pose (Baddha Konasana)

Sit with soles of feet together, gently flapping your knees like butterfly wings.

8. Boat Pose (Paripurna Navasana)

Balance on your sitting bones, lifting legs and chest to make a strong boat.

9. Star Pose (Utthita Tadasana)

Stand wide-legged with arms outstretched, shining bright like a star.

10. Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana)

Lie on your back and lift your hips, making a sturdy bridge for cars to cross.

For Teens & Adults (Ages 13-60)

These poses focus on building strength, flexibility, balance, and mindfulness for a deeper practice.

1. Warrior I (Virabhadrasana I)

Ground yourself with strong legs, lifting your arms with determination.

2. Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II)

Open your hips and gaze forward, embodying strength and focus.

3. Triangle Pose (Trikonasana)

Extend through your side body, finding length and stability.

4. Chair Pose (Utkatasana)

Sit deep into an imaginary chair, building strength in your legs.

5. Plank Pose (Phalakasana)

Hold a straight line from head to heels, engaging your core.

6. Tree Pose (Vrksasana)

Find your balance and inner calm, rooted like a strong tree.

7. Crescent Lunge (Anjaneyasana)

Step forward, open your chest, and reach for the sky, like a crescent moon.

8. Dancer's Pose (Natarajasana)

Balance and extend, feeling the grace and power of a dancer.

9. Happy Baby Pose (Ananda Balasana)

Gently pull your knees to your chest, feeling playful and relaxed like a happy baby.

10. Savasana (Corpse Pose)

Lie still and completely relax, letting your body absorb the benefits of your practice.

For Seniors & Gentle Yoga (Ages 60+)

Gentle movements, balance, and joint mobility are key. Many poses can be adapted with chair support.

1. Seated Mountain Pose (Chair Tadasana)

Sit tall in a chair, feet flat, feeling grounded and present.

2. Seated Cat-Cow (Chair Marjaryasana-Bitilasana)

Gently arch and round your spine while seated, improving flexibility.

3. Chair Tree Pose (Chair Vrksasana)

Practice balance by placing one foot on your ankle or calf, using chair support.

4. Seated Forward Fold (Chair Paschimottanasana)

Gently fold forward from your hips while seated, releasing tension in your back.

5. Seated Spinal Twist (Chair Ardha Matsyendrasana)

Twist gently to open your spine and improve mobility, using the chair for support.

6. Leg Raises (Seated/Supine)

Gently lift one leg at a time to strengthen your core and leg muscles.

7. Ankle Rotations (Seated)

Rotate your ankles in circles to improve joint mobility and circulation.

8. Shoulder Rolls (Seated/Standing)

Roll your shoulders forward and backward to release tension in your upper back.

9. Wall Push-ups (Wall Phalakasana variation)

Build upper body strength safely by pressing against a wall.

10. Supported Savasana (with pillows/blankets)

Relax completely, using props for comfort, allowing your body to rest deeply.
